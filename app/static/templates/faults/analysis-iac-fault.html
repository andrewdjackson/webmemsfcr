Also known as stepper motor idle air control valve (IACV)
The stepper motor bolts on the side of the injection body housing to control engine idle speed and air flow from cold start up
A high number of steps indicates that the ECU is attempting to close the stepper motor and reduce the airflow
A low number would indicate the inability to open the stepper motor and increase airflow
IAC position invalid if the idle offset exceeds the max error, yet the IAC Position remains at 0

It's a common fault where the stepper motor wears and begin to skip steps.
A poorly adjusted IAC setting can also have a dramatic effect.
It is vitally important that the correct procedure with a diagnostic tools such as MemsFCR are followed to adjust the IAC / Stepper Motor
